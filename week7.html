Industrial Automation System: What is it?
Types and Benefits

5 min read | Schneider Electric
Original text from: Schneider Electric Blog

What is an automation system?

The basic definition of industrial automation can be described as a set of computerized systems
which are found in a manufacturing facility. These systems can be seen as a way to reduce human
labour and automate mundane, mechanical, and repetitive tasks.
The automation systems in the industry have evolved.
As a result, different industrial automation systems are available today.
The main goal of these systems is to make industries' processes more efficient and save costs by minimizing human errors.
With the rise of modernization and technological advances, almost every industry is evolving, and the manufacturing industry is not far behind.
This sector has seen large-scale adoption of these automation systems to increase effectiveness and productivity in facilities.
In fact, the adoption is such that the industrial automation industry has seen unprecedented growth over the last decade.
As per the data released by Fortune Business Insights, the global industrial automation market is projected to grow to a staggering $296.70 billion by 2026.
These numbers show real promise for the future of the industrial automation sector.
However, many firms are still determining if this technological advancement is right for them.
Therefore, in order to answer and clarify all doubts related to industrial automation and its undisputed benefits,
we will highlight some of the most prominent types of industrial automation systems found in various manufacturing facilities.

What is system automation?

System automation refers to the use of technology to control and operate processes without human intervention.
It integrates software, hardware, and other components to perform specific tasks, improving efficiency, reliability, and accuracy.
Automation can be applied to various industries, from manufacturing to IT, streamlining operations and reducing manual effort.

Types of industrial automation systems

Fixed automation

A fixed automation system is also known as hardwired automation. This type is used for high-volume production, repetitive tasks, and products with a long-life cycle. Though it is tricky to accommodate changes in product design with this type, it helps cut time and labour costs.

Examples of fixed automation systems:

  • Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) based systems
  • Human Machine Interface (HMI), which provides operators with process information via displays

Flexible automation

This system is the most commonly used. It utilizes programmable logic controllers (PLCs) that automate a wide range of control tasks.
It allows a more flexible production process using programmable logic and networking.
Examples include automatic guided vehicles, automobiles, and CNC machinery.
It's suitable for batch processing, discrete manufacturing, and job shops.

Programmable automation

Best for batch production with medium to high volume. The system must be reprogrammed and reconfigured between batches, which takes time. Examples include CNC machines, PLCs, and industrial robots.

Benefits of an industrial automation system

Technologies in industrial automation have evolved with the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT). This has enabled firms to unlock significant benefits:

Enhanced quality

Automation ensures consistency and fewer human errors, resulting in higher-quality products.

Better efficiency and higher output

Robots can work 24/7 and produce more efficiently than human operators.

Cost efficient

Though robots require an upfront investment, they reduce long-term labour and operational costs.

Safer operation

Automation minimizes human exposure to dangerous environments, improving workplace safety.
